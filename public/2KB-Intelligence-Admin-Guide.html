1. Platform Overview & Architecture

2KB Intelligence is a unified energy engineering command center designed for managing Energy Savings Performance Contracts (ESPCs) from prospect through closeout. The platform serves as a single pane of glass for energy auditors, owner's representatives, construction managers, and project leads.

Core Capabilities

Portfolio Management

Track multiple ESPC projects across all lifecycle phases with real-time KPIs, risk scoring, and pipeline visibility.

Field Audit & AI Extraction

Capture equipment data via camera, transcribe audio notes, and use AI to auto-extract asset attributes from photos.

Financial Modeling

Build ECM bundles, model cash flows, compute payback/NPV/DSCR, and compare pricing against historical benchmarks.

Governance & Compliance

Full audit trail on every edit, data locking, role-based permissions, and contractual obligation tracking.

2. Getting Started & Onboarding

When an administrator first logs in with no projects configured, the platform displays an Onboarding Guide in place of the empty dashboard. This guide disappears automatically once the first project is created or imported.

First-Time Setup Steps

  1. Create Your First Project — Navigate to the Projects page and set up an ESPC project with building info, ESCO details, phase, and value.
  2. Import Project Data — Use the Import Project File modal to upload an Excel workbook. The system auto-detects sheet types (assets, utility bills, ECMs, milestones) and routes data to the correct modules.
  3. Review & Benchmark — Verify imported data, flag equipment deficiencies, and open Energy Benchmarking to review baseline EUI and consumption trends.
  4. Set Up Workflows — Assign tasks to team members, set due dates, and establish milestone tracking for project deliverables.
Quick Start for Power Users If you already have a completed project workbook (Excel), click Import Project File in the sidebar to skip manual entry. The importer auto-maps columns and populates all modules at once.

3. Authentication & User Management

Login

Access the platform at the root URL and select Engineer Login. Enter your email and password. Authentication tokens are stored in the browser session; closing the tab ends the session.

User Roles

RoleDescriptionKey Permissions
Engineer Field staff and technical contributors View all data, edit own records, capture assets, run benchmarks
Project Lead Project managers overseeing deliverables All Engineer permissions + edit any record, approve reports, manage milestones
Admin Platform administrators with full access All Project Lead permissions + unlock records, delete data, manage users, configure settings

Per-Project Role Overrides

Users can hold different roles on different projects. For example, an Engineer on one project may be the Project Lead on another. Admins configure this in Settings → User Management.

User Switching (Demo Mode)

Click your avatar in the bottom-left sidebar to reveal the user switcher. Select a different team member to view the platform from their role perspective. This feature is useful for testing permission boundaries.

7. Field Audit & Asset Intelligence

The Field Audit module enables field engineers to capture, manage, and analyze building equipment during on-site energy audits.

Asset Capture

  • Camera Capture — Take photos of equipment directly from the browser. Photos are stored and linked to asset records.
  • Audio Transcription — Record voice notes during walkthroughs. AI transcribes and extracts equipment details.
  • AI Photo Extraction — Upload equipment photos and the AI vision model identifies manufacturer, model, serial number, year, capacity, and condition.

Asset Database

All captured equipment is stored in a searchable, sortable table with the following fields:

FieldDescription
TypeEquipment category (HVAC, Lighting, Controls, Envelope, Plumbing, etc.)
ManufacturerEquipment manufacturer
ModelModel number
Year InstalledInstallation year
ConditionGood / Fair / Poor / Failed
Remaining Useful Life (RUL)Estimated years remaining
Replacement CostEstimated cost to replace ($)
Deficiency FlagsR-22 refrigerant, age exceeds EUL, low SEER/AFUE, etc.
AI ConfidenceConfidence score from AI extraction (0–100%)

Deficiency Flagging Rules

Automatic Deficiency Detection The rules engine automatically flags assets based on: R-22 refrigerant use (regulatory phaseout), equipment age exceeding expected useful life, SEER rating below current standards, AFUE below efficiency thresholds, and remaining useful life ≤ 0.

8. Energy Benchmarking

The Benchmarking module provides energy performance analysis using utility consumption data, building characteristics, and industry standards.

Key Features

  • Energy Use Intensity (EUI) — Calculates kBtu/sqft/yr normalized by building area. Compares against ENERGY STAR and CBECS baselines.
  • Utility Consumption Trends — Monthly electricity (kWh), natural gas (therms), and water charts with year-over-year comparison.
  • Cost Analysis — $/sqft energy cost, blended rate tracking, peak demand charges.
  • Capital Planning Timeline — 15-year asset replacement schedule based on equipment end-of-life dates, visualized as a horizontal timeline chart.
  • Building Comparison — Side-by-side EUI comparison across multiple buildings within a project.

Utility Bill Import

Utility bills can be imported individually or in bulk via the SharePoint Import modal. The system accepts monthly data with fields for billing period, consumption (kWh/therms), cost, and peak demand. Data freshness is tracked with configurable amber (45 days) and red (75 days) thresholds.

9. Financial Modeling & ESPC Structuring

ECM Management

Energy Conservation Measures (ECMs) are the building blocks of ESPC financial models. Each ECM includes:

FieldDescription
CategoryHVAC, Lighting, Controls, Envelope, Water, Renewables
DescriptionWhat the measure involves
Installation CostTotal installed cost ($)
Annual SavingsProjected yearly savings ($)
Measure LifeExpected lifespan in years
Simple PaybackAuto-calculated: Cost / Annual Savings

Financial Outputs

  • Bundle Analysis — Group ECMs into packages and evaluate combined economics.
  • Cash Flow Projections — Year-by-year savings vs. debt service with adjustable term, interest rate, and escalation factors.
  • Net Present Value (NPV) — Discounted total savings over contract term.
  • Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR) — Ensures savings exceed annual payments. Target ≥ 1.25x.
  • Scenario Comparison — Model multiple scenarios with different ECM bundles, contract terms, or interest rates.
  • Pricing Review — Compare proposed ECM costs against historical benchmarks from the Knowledge Base.

12. Measurement & Verification (M&V)

The M&V module tracks whether installed ECMs are achieving their guaranteed energy savings during the performance period.

Core Metrics

MetricDescription
Guaranteed Savings ($)Contractually guaranteed annual savings amount
Calculated Savings ($)Actual measured/calculated savings for the period
Achievement (%)Calculated / Guaranteed × 100
Drift DetectedBoolean flag if performance deviates beyond threshold

Drift Detection

Automatic Drift Alerts When calculated savings fall more than 5% below the guaranteed amount, the system flags a drift event. Drift alerts appear on the Dashboard alert strip and in M&V KPIs. Root cause investigation should examine equipment performance, operational changes, weather normalization, and occupancy shifts.

M&V Workflow

  1. Import Annual Data — Upload consumption data for the measurement period.
  2. Baseline Adjustment — Apply weather normalization and occupancy corrections.
  3. Calculate Savings — Compare adjusted consumption against baseline.
  4. Review & Certify — Project lead reviews calculations and certifies annual M&V report.
  5. Track Trends — Multi-year savings trend with year-over-year comparison.

Report Lifecycle

  1. Draft — Author creates report, auto-populated from project data.
  2. In Review — Submitted for QA review. Reviewer checks against QA checklist.
  3. QA Complete — All checklist items verified. Comments resolved.
  4. Approved — Project Lead or Admin approves. Record locked. Approval timestamp and approver logged.

QA Checklist

Each report type has a configurable QA checklist. Reviewers check items off as they verify accuracy. Comments can be added to flag issues, and must be resolved before approval.

Council Presentation Slides

Auto-generated presentation content organized into sections: Project Overview, Savings Performance, Financial Summary, Risk Assessment, and Carbon Impact. Designed for board-level stakeholders.

20. Data Freshness System

The platform monitors how recently each data module has been updated and alerts administrators when data becomes stale.

Freshness Thresholds

ModuleAmber ThresholdRed ThresholdMeaning
Utility Bills45 days75 daysMonthly billing data should be current
Assets90 days90 daysEquipment data refreshed after audits
M&V45 days75 daysPerformance data during M&V period
Financial180 days180 daysFinancial models updated semi-annually
Risk30 days30 daysRisk register reviewed monthly
Inspection14 days14 daysActive construction requires frequent updates

Freshness Indicators

  • Fresh — Data updated within the amber threshold. No action needed.
  • Stale — Data age exceeds amber threshold but not red. Refresh recommended.
  • Overdue — Data age exceeds red threshold. Immediate update required.

Where Freshness Appears

  • Sidebar navigation — Colored dots next to module names with stale data.
  • Dashboard Data Health panel — Lists all stale modules with days since last update.
  • Top alert bar — Red banner appears when any module exceeds its red threshold.
  • Freshness Badge component — Inline indicator on detail pages.

25. Glossary & Reference

TermDefinition
ESPCEnergy Savings Performance Contract — a funding mechanism where an ESCO guarantees energy savings to finance building improvements.
ESCOEnergy Service Company — the contractor who designs, installs, and guarantees energy savings.
ECMEnergy Conservation Measure — a specific upgrade (e.g., LED retrofit, chiller replacement) designed to reduce energy consumption.
EUIEnergy Use Intensity — a building's annual energy consumption normalized by floor area (kBtu/sqft/yr).
IGEAInvestment Grade Energy Audit — a detailed facility assessment that produces specific ECM recommendations with financial analysis.
M&VMeasurement & Verification — the process of quantifying actual energy savings achieved after ECM installation.
DSCRDebt Service Coverage Ratio — annual savings divided by annual debt payments. Target ≥ 1.25x.
NPVNet Present Value — the discounted total benefit of a project over its lifetime.
RULRemaining Useful Life — estimated years before equipment needs replacement.
SEERSeasonal Energy Efficiency Ratio — cooling efficiency rating for HVAC equipment.
AFUEAnnual Fuel Utilization Efficiency — heating efficiency rating for furnaces/boilers.
R-22HCFC-22 refrigerant — being phased out under EPA regulations. Presence is a deficiency flag.
CBECSCommercial Buildings Energy Consumption Survey — DOE dataset used for benchmarking.
tCO2eTonnes of CO2 equivalent — standard unit for greenhouse gas emissions.
DriftDeviation of actual savings from guaranteed savings during the M&V period.
Owner's Rep (OR)Owner's Representative — acts on behalf of the building owner to oversee the ESCO's performance.
